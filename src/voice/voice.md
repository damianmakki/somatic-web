# Somatic — Voice & Tone Guide

Use this as a reference before writing any blog post, caption, or product copy. The goal is consistency across all content: every piece should feel like it came from the same person.

---

## The Voice in One Sentence

Write like someone who has read the sports science *and* actually lifts — not a researcher, not a bro. Knowledgeable without being clinical. Direct without being aggressive.

The reader should feel like they're getting the honest version, not the sold version.

---

## Core Characteristics

### Dry and grounded
Understatement over enthusiasm. Trust the reader to feel the implication without you spelling it out.

> ✅ "You've been meaning to get it looked at. You probably won't."
> ❌ "So many people struggle to prioritize addressing their nagging injuries!"

---

### Rhythm is intentional
Vary sentence length deliberately. Longer sentences build context. Short ones land the point. The punch comes from contrast, not word choice.

> ✅ "The right ratio of stress to recovery is where progress actually lives. Miss the recovery side of that equation long enough and the stress stops building you up. It just wears you down."
> ❌ "It's important to maintain a proper balance between stress and recovery in order to continue making progress over time."

---

### Specific over general
Concrete details create recognition. Abstract statements create nothing. If you're going to make a claim, put a face on it.

> ✅ "The shoulder that's been a little off for three months."
> ❌ "Many people deal with nagging injuries that go unaddressed."

---

### Honest about the industry — once
One clean observation and move on. Don't dwell on who's wrong. The post is too busy being useful.

> ✅ "That culture is great for selling supplements. It's terrible for actually getting stronger over time."
> ❌ Two paragraphs on hustle culture and why it's harmful.

---

### The product earns its place
Somatic and Soma only appear when the writing has already made the case for why something like them matters. The product is never the subject of a sentence — it's the logical next step of one. One mention mid-post (woven into context), one mention at the end (as a CTA). That's it.

> ✅ "This is the thing Somatic is built around. Before Soma suggests your next session, it reads your recent training history..."
> ❌ "Somatic is an AI-powered fitness app that helps you train smarter by analyzing your recovery data."

---

## What to Avoid

| Avoid | Why |
|---|---|
| "The myth is X. The reality is Y." | Listicle framing — sounds generated |
| Wrapping every section with a tidy summary | Kills the breathing room |
| FAQ sections as filler | If it matters, put it in the body |
| Enthusiasm words: *game-changer, powerful, incredible, transformative* | Vague and overused |
| Hedging phrases: *"it's important to note," "it's worth mentioning"* | Padding — just say the thing |
| Addressing the reader as a demographic: *"for athletes and gym-goers alike"* | Othering — write to one person |
| Numbered contrast markers: *"01 / 02 / 03"* | Template feel, only use if content is a true sequence |
| Ending on the CTA alone | Earn the CTA — close the argument first, then link |

## Structure Principles

**Open with a scene, not a stat.**
Start with something the reader recognizes — a feeling, a situation, a thing they've been ignoring. Pull them in with specificity, *then* back it up with data.

**Let sections breathe.**
Not every section needs a conclusion sentence. Sometimes the last line of a section is the point, and adding another sentence after it just dilutes it.

**Subheadings are signposts, not titles.**
They should tell the reader where they're going, not summarize what they just read. Keep them short and plain.

**Sub-items use bold lead-ins, not `###` headers.**
Within a section that has multiple points, write them as `**bold lead-in**` followed by a paragraph — not as `### subheadings`. Subheadings create a slide-deck feel; bold lead-ins stay inside the flow of a single voice.

> ✅ `**Check in before you load the bar**`
> ❌ `### Check in before you load the bar`

**Citations anchor credibility — don't weaponize them.**
One or two well-placed references do more than a footnote on every paragraph. The writing should stand on its own; the citations confirm it.

---

## The Somatic/Soma Distinction

- **Somatic** = the product/app. Use for brand-level mentions.
- **Soma** = the AI inside the app. Use when describing what it actually does in a session.

> "Somatic is built around this idea. Soma reads your training history and recovery signals before every suggestion."

---

## CTA Format

Keep it minimal. No hard sell. The post has already done the work.

```
*No subscription. Your data never leaves your iPhone.* [Join the beta via TestFlight.](https://testflight.apple.com/join/CVT6dz4U)
```

Italic for the trust line. Linked text for the action. One sentence total.

---

## Quick Self-Check Before Publishing

Before you post, read it back and ask:

- Does the opener make someone recognize themselves?
- Is there at least one sentence that couldn't have been written by anyone else?
- Does Somatic/Soma appear more than twice?
- Are there any sentences that exist just to transition? (Delete them.)
- Would a smart, tired person keep reading after the first paragraph?
